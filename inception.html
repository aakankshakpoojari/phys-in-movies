Poetic Ending

Inception introduces the idea that time flows differently in dreams. While dreams may seem longer than reality, the film exaggerates this effect significantly. The film ends with Cobb spinning his totem. If it falls, he’s in the real world. If it keeps spinning forever, he’s still dreaming. But before we get the answer, the screen cuts to black. That final moment sparked endless debates — was it real or not? Nolan never confirms it, because the point is: Cobb doesn’t care anymore. He’s finally at peace.
